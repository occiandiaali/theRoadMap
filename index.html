That's always the problem. Experience.

But how many years of experience did Mark have before FaceBook? Or did Jeff, before Amazon.

Iyinoluwa co-founded Andela in 2014 shortly after graduation, and 2 years later co-founded Flutterwave. Both are known unicorns.

Jack came up with the idea for Twitter while he was a student at NYU. Evan Spiegel's Snapchat was a product design class project. Max Levchin (PayPal co-founder) started a company with 2 fellow University of Illinois students before coming up with PayPal. Elon co-founded Zip2 in the same year he graduated from UPenn.

These individuals had just about (or even less) the same years of experience as you. But they chose not to suffer the grind of the apply->be-anxious->get-rejected vicious cycle. Why don't you choose the same?
